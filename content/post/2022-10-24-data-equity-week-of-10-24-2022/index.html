Beneficence: be transparent about limits of data

Our data has been collected by the MLB over a long historical period. Although we will be exclusively using data relevant to the past ten years, we are limited to the reporting of the MLB matching up with weather data. Since weather data is specific to location, we are limited to focusing on select teams solely playing at home instead of the MLB as a whole. Since we will be collecting weather data from the city as a whole, it may not be the most accurate representation of the exact weather conditions at the stadium; based on the proximity of the weather station to the stadium itself. Additional factors outside of game data and weather such as random stadium factors will not be a part of our analysis.

Justice: Return data and research results to community members in a form they can use

Our research results should be useful to MLB teams, or at least the specific teams we focus on, to allow for better anticipatory guidance in the pursuit of winning games. We seek to explore what factors–either internal or external–affect the outcomes of baseball games, which would be useful to know going into a season. Even if we pick to concentrate on one or a few teams/cities, our research methodology should be straightforward and replicable, so any other team could easily attain such results.

Please read the this article on Data for Equity Principals. While all aspects of this are not relevant for your projects, and the relevance may vary for depending on your chosen data sets, there are some important things to think about in general about collecting and working with data.
